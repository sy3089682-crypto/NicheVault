# Niche Report: Cursive-to-Context (AI for Local Historical Societies)

## Description
A specialized software service bundle for small-town local historical societies and genealogy clubs. It uses high-accuracy "Cursive OCR" (trained on 18th-19th century handwriting) to digitize physical records (deeds, letters, ledger books) and automatically cross-references names and dates with local cemetery and census records.

## Why it has zero/low competition
- **The "Trunk Data" Gap:** Platforms like Ancestry.com focus on massive, government-level datasets. They don't touch the "box in the attic" of a local society.
- **Local Context Requirement:** General OCR tools fail on handwritten local names and locations. This service uses local RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to verify names against local town lists.
- **Volunteer Fatigue:** Most local societies are run by seniors who are overwhelmed by boxes of physical records but have high "passion-to-budget" ratios.

## Demand signals
- **The "Legacy" Trend:** Boomers are downsizing and donating literal tons of physical documents to local societies, creating a massive administrative backlog.
- **Gen Z Heritage Reclaiming:** Surge in interest in "Hyper-Localism" and genealogical roots on TikTok/Instagram.
- **Grant Availability:** National and state-level historical preservation grants are increasingly prioritizing "Digital Access" projects.

## Estimated willingness to pay
- **Project-Based:** $2,500 - $5,000 for a "Box Digitization Sprint."
- **SaaS Membership:** $49/month for hosting the town's digital archive and allowing residents to search.

## First-mover advantages
- **The Local Knowledge Graph:** Building a proprietary database of how names and families connected in specific rural counties, which becomes more valuable than broad census data.
- **Trust with "Gatekeepers":** Building relationships with the aging leaders of these societies.

## Recommended go-to-market approach
- **Conference Circuit:** Booths at the various State Historical Society conferences.
- **Local Sponsorship:** Offer one town a free digitization project in exchange for a case study to show at the state level.
- **Grant Writing as a Service:** Help the society apply for the grant that pays for your tool.
