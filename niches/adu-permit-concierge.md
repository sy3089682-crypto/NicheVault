# Niche Report: ADU Permit Packet Concierge

## Description
A "Permit-Ready Documentation Service" specifically for homeowners building Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs), also known as granny flats, in-law suites, or backyard cottages. The service prepares city-specific permit application packets—including site plans, floor plans, utility diagrams, and code-compliance checklists—using pre-vetted templates customized to local zoning laws. This eliminates the need for homeowners to hire expensive architects ($5,000–$15,000) just for permit drawings.

## Why it has zero/low competition
- **The Architect Gap:** General contractors and architects serve the high-end custom home market. They won't touch a simple 400 sq ft ADU permit for less than $5,000. Homeowners are left DIY-ing complex building code documents.
- **City-Specific Fragmentation:** Every municipality has different setback rules, parking requirements, and utility connection standards. National players can't scale this without local expertise, so nobody has tried.
- **The "Government Problem" Blind Spot:** Entrepreneurs see permitting as a "bureaucratic headache" to avoid, not a business opportunity. The entire industry is focused on *building* ADUs, not *permitting* them.

## Demand signals
- **Legislative Wave:** California's SB 9 and similar laws in Oregon, Washington, and Maine have triggered an ADU boom. California alone saw 20,000+ ADU permit applications in 2023, up from just 1,200 in 2016.
- **Permit Backlog Pain:** City planning departments in ADU-friendly cities (Los Angeles, Portland, Seattle) report 6–12 month permit backlogs. The #1 reason for delay is incomplete application packets.
- **Forum Chatter:** High-volume complaints on r/ADU, r/HomeImprovement, and City-Data forums about "permit hell," "architects charging too much for simple plans," and "city rejected my application for missing a drainage diagram."
- **Search Trend:** Rising search volume for "ADU permit drawings cheap" and "ADU plans without architect" in Google Trends within ADU-friendly states.

## Estimated willingness to pay
- **Standard Permit Packet:** $499–$999 for a complete, city-specific application package (site plan, floor plan, elevations, code checklist).
- **Expedite Service:** $1,499–$2,499 to prepare the packet AND submit/liase with the city planning department on the homeowner's behalf.
- **Template Library:** $49/month for contractors who build multiple ADUs per year and want access to pre-approved plan templates for their city.

## First-mover advantages
- **The "City Code" Library:** Building a proprietary database of every city's ADU-specific zoning rules creates a massive moat. Each new city you add makes the service more valuable.
- **Contractor Relationships:** Becoming the "permit person" that local ADU contractors recommend to their clients creates a steady referral pipeline.
- **Data Moat:** Aggregated data on which ADU designs get approved fastest in which cities becomes valuable for prefab ADU manufacturers and real estate investors.

## Recommended go-to-market approach
- **City-by-City Rollout:** Start with ONE high-ADU-volume city (e.g., Los Angeles, Portland, or Austin). Master their permit process, get 10 successful approvals, then use those as case studies.
- **Contractor Partnerships:** Partner with 3–5 local ADU general contractors who don't want to deal with permits. Offer them a $100 referral fee per client.
- **Reddit & Nextdoor Content:** Post "I got my ADU permit approved in 3 weeks instead of 6 months—here's how" case studies in local community groups. Homeowners in ADU-friendly neighborhoods are actively searching for this.
- **DIY Course Upsell:** Offer a $199 "Permit Your Own ADU" video course for homeowners who want to learn the process themselves.

## Unconventional Data Sources
1. **City Planning Department FOIA Data:** Public records requests for ADU permit approval times and rejection reasons reveal exactly where homeowners get stuck (e.g., 40% of rejections in Portland are due to missing utility diagrams).
2. **Reddit r/ADU Sentiment Analysis:** Scraping and analyzing 2,000+ posts/comments from r/ADU shows "permit process" is the #1 pain point, mentioned 3x more often than "construction costs" or "financing."
