## Email 2: Unconventional Research Method (Send Day 2)

**Subject:** The "review archaeology" trick that finds niches in 20 minutes

**Preview text:** Everyone uses Google Trends. Almost no one does this.

---

Hey [First Name],

Yesterday we showed you a $120M niche with zero competition.

Today, I want to show you *exactly* how we found it—so you can find your own.

Most people hunt niches with Google Trends, SEMrush, and Ahrefs. Those tools are fine. They're also where everyone else looks.

Here's our favorite unconventional method. We call it **review archaeology**.

### Step 1: Pick an adjacent market

Don't search for your niche directly. Search for the *category above* it.

For pet bereavement, we didn't search "pet grief app" (there are no results—because no app exists). Instead, we searched **"grief app"** and **"journaling app."**

### Step 2: Read the 1-star and 3-star reviews

Not the 5-stars (those are useless). Not the 2-stars (those are just angry people).

The **3-star reviews** are gold. They're from people who *wanted* the product to work but it didn't quite fit.

In the reviews for Mend (a general grief app), we found this pattern repeated dozens of times:

> *"This is great for losing a parent, but I just lost my dog of 14 years and none of these prompts fit."*

> *"I wish there was an option to say I'm grieving a pet, not a person."*

### Step 3: Follow the forum signal

Take those complaints to Reddit. Search the exact phrases reviewers used.

When we searched "app for pet loss" on Reddit, we found r/PetLoss threads with hundreds of upvotes and *zero solution recommendations*.

That combination—**paid-app demand + forum desperation + no existing product**—is our signal.

### Step 4: Validate with trends

Only after steps 1-3 do we check Google Trends. And when we did, "pet loss journal" was up +340%.

The demand was already there. The supply was not.

---

**Your homework:** Pick any app category you're slightly interested in. Read 20 three-star reviews. Look for the phrase *"I wish this had..."* or *"This is great for X but not for Y."*

That's your niche signal.

Hit reply and tell me what you find. I read every email.
